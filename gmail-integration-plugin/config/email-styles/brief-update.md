---
name: "Brief Update"
description: "When to use: Quick status updates, progress reports, or simple information sharing where brevity is essential. Works for any professional relationship where context is already established."
---

<examples>
Quick update: Project completed on schedule. All deliverables submitted to client.

Next: Debrief meeting scheduled for Monday.

Warren
---
Status update on the proposal:
- Draft completed
- Legal review in progress
- Target submission: Friday

Will send final version once approved.

Thanks,
Warren
</examples>

<greeting>
- Skip greeting or use brief "Hi,"
- Jump straight to content
</greeting>

<body>
- Extremely concise
- Bullet points preferred
- Action items clearly stated
- Context assumed (established thread)
</body>

<closing>
- Minimal: just name or "Thanks,"
- Can omit entirely for very brief updates
</closing>

<do>
- Be extremely concise
- Use bullet points for multiple items
- Lead with most important information
- Include clear next steps if applicable
</do>

<dont>
- Add unnecessary context or pleasantries
- Write full paragraphs when bullets work
- Use for complex topics needing discussion
- Forget to specify timeline/deadlines
</dont>
