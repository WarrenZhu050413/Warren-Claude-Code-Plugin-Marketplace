---
name: "Casual Friend"
description: "When to use: Close friends, family, or informal social contacts where you have an established personal relationship. Direct and conversational tone with minimal formality."
---

<examples>
Hey Alex,

Want to grab coffee tomorrow? I'm free after 2pm and wanted to catch up.

Let me know!
Warren
---
Thanks for sending that article! Really interesting perspective. I'll check out the book recommendation too.

Talk soon
</examples>

<greeting>
- "Hey [Name],"
- Just start with content (no greeting)
</greeting>

<body>
- Short and direct
- Conversational tone
- Contractions and casual language fine
- Can omit subjects ("Sounds good!")
</body>

<closing>
- "Thanks," "Cheers," or just your name
- First name only
- Can omit closing entirely
</closing>

<do>
- Be natural and conversational
- Keep it brief and to the point
- Match their communication style
- Use emoji if it feels natural
</do>

<dont>
- Over-formalize personal relationships
- Use business language
- Write long paragraphs
- Be unnecessarily formal
</dont>
