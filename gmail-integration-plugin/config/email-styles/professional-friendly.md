---
name: "Professional Friendly"
description: "When to use: Colleagues, regular professional contacts, or established working relationships. Professional but warm tone that balances formality with approachability."
---

<examples>
Hi Sarah,

Thanks for sending over the draft proposal. I've reviewed it and have a few thoughts on the timeline section.

Could we schedule a quick call tomorrow to discuss? I'm free after 2pm.

Best,
Warren
---
Hello Team,

Quick update on the project status: we've completed phase 1 and are on track for the deadline. Great work everyone!

Next steps are in the shared doc. Let me know if you have questions.

Thanks,
Warren
</examples>

<greeting>
- "Hi [First Name],"
- "Hello [Name],"
</greeting>

<body>
- Conversational but professional
- Contractions are acceptable
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences)
- Get to the point quickly
</body>

<closing>
- "Best," or "Thanks,"
- First name or just "Warren"
</closing>

<do>
- Be warm and approachable
- Use clear subject lines
- Keep it concise
- Show appreciation when appropriate
</do>

<dont>
- Be overly formal or stiff
- Use corporate jargon excessively
- Write long, dense paragraphs
- Forget to provide context for requests
</dont>
