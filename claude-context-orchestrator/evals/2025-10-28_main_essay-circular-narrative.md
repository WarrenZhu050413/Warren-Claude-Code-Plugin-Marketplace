## Summary

Modified developing-essays skill to address two critical gaps:
1. **Circular narrative structure** - Essays that establish opening themes but fail to close the loop in conclusions
2. **Opening hooks** - Weak openings that start with unmotivated quotes or generic statements

## Key Discoveries

### 1. Circular Narrative Gap
**Problem**: Students end essays with beautiful realizations that have no connection to opening themes. Example: Opening says "grown through words of others," but conclusion just says "bridge-building is carried in everyday objects."

**Solution**: Added "Circular Narrative Structure" subsection to Forward Projection section with:
- Opening → Conclusion Circle template
- Test for checking natural connection
- Example showing weak vs. strong conclusions
- Explicit callback requirement

### 2. Realization → Action Gap
**Problem**: Essays frequently end with "I realized X" instead of "Because I realized X, I now do Y when Z happens."

**Solution**: Added "Realization → Action Template" subsection to Actionability Test with:
- Pattern: "I realized [insight]. Now when [situation], I [specific behavior]"
- Examples contrasting weak (realization only) vs. strong (realization + trigger + behavior)
- Test requiring both trigger situation AND specific behavior

### 3. Opening Hook Weakness
**Problem**: Essays start with unmotivated quotes or generic statements that don't establish stakes or engage readers.

**Solution**: Added "Opening Strategies for Bridge-Building Essays" section with:
- 4 hook types (surprising moment, tension, vivid scene, confession)
- Before/after examples showing how to setup quotes with context
- Test: "Could essay start at paragraph 2?"
- Common mistakes to avoid
